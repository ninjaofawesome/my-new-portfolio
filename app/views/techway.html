Here's What Happened.

The Setup

Techway was a group extern project that I had the pleasure of working on while I was attending Flatiron. It was meant to give us valuable experience working with clients and rails, while giving a service to a startup who might not otherwise be able to afford a custom built project.

For this project, we met with a local PC repair company run by Jamel Graham. When we met Mr. Graham, he had a site that wasnâ€™t really fitting the needs of his small company- it was hard for him to get clientele to contact him, difficult to maintain his records and update services as he needed.

The Plan Of Attack

After consulting with Mr. Graham, we determined that he would benefit most from a custom built content management system that had administrative and client side features without many bells and whistles. On the client side, he needed a form for submissions, a form reviews, a list of services and (as it was important to Mr. Graham) positive reviews. On the admin side, Mr. Graham needed a way to organize client records, choose reviews to feature on the landing page and have the ability to update services.

We used Rails 3.2 for this project and minimally used gems for components for a richer learning experience.

The Solution

Working with a group of three, we built him a content management system which met all of his needs. We deployed his project to Heroku for staging and set up a server on Digital Ocean for production. Our project was completed on time, with all parameters met.

One of the best parts of working on Techway is that with a group of three other students, we were able to quad program, each taking turns to drive the programming and to find solutions for areas we got stuck in collaboratively.
