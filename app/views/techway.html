Here's What Happened.

The Setup

Techway was a group extern project that I had the pleasure of working on at The Flatiron School. It was meant to give us valuable experience working with clients who might not be able to afford a dev shop to build features, as well as more experience with Rails.

For this project, we met with a local PC repair company run by Jamel Graham. When we met Mr. Graham, he had another handmade site that wasn’t really fitting the needs of his small company- it was hard for him to get clientele to contact him, difficult to maintain his records or update services.

The Plan Of Attack

After consulting with him, our team determined that he would benefit most from a custom built CMS that had bell-and-whistle free administrative and client side features. On the client side, he needed a form for submissions, a form for clients to submit reviews, a list of services he could update, a way to organize and update client information and a way to cherry pick reviews to be featured on the landing page.

We used Rails 3.2 for this and used some popular gems (such as Devise for user authentication), but most components were built by hand otherwise for a richer learning experience. We divided each point of the criteria into features and “quad programmed” (one driver, three contributors) on each.

The Solution

In a month, our team was able to build Mr. Graham a CMS that met all his needs for administrators and also created a clean, timeless front end to display it to the public. We also successfully deployed the site to Heroku for a staging area and created a server on Digital Ocean for the production version.

One of the best parts of working on Techway is that with a group of three other students, we were able to form a supportive team to produce a unique product. We learned much about efficient coding, version control and how to work with nontechnical clients in a way that showcased our natural talents and newly learned ones.
